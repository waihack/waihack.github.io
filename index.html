THE COLLEGE
HACKATHON

In the context of a hackathon, the word "hack" is used to describe how multiple technologies can be used together in a new and innovative way. Teams of up to four people spend the weekend working on innovative software and hardware solutions to real-world problems. These projects range in platform and application, including elements of web development, mobile applications, drones, blockchain, and more. However, many times the most important aspect of a hackathon is the community it generates and skills that inexperienced hackers walk away with.








FAQ

Travel

How much does it cost?

Nada. Zip. Zero. Zilch. We'll provide you with a weekend's worth of swag, meals, drinks, and snacks and a place to crash when you need a break from coding. In fact, we'll even reimburse your travel. You will be able to indicate travel reumbersment in your application's confirmation form.

How do reimbursements work?

When you're accepted, we'll have a page where you can submit your required travel reimbursement forms and receipts. For each location hackers will be coming from, we determine the cheapest reasonable option and set a reimbursement cap at a bit more than the cost of that option. We will reimburse all travel costs up to this cap—if you go over, we'll still reimburse the amount of the cap, but no more.

Will there be transportation to Waikato Uni?

Not at this stage. Busses may be arranged from key universities in the North Island to Waikato Uni. Information about this will be released closer to the event. For the time being attendees are expected to find their own transportation to the event. For North Island attendees we advise using one of the bus services to hamilton transport service. We will be running vans between Hamilton transport center and Waikato University on the day of the event. Information about these vans will be released closer to the time of the event. If you indicate in your confirmation form, we can find a Waikato Student to give you a ride between Hamilton Transport Center and the University

Once I am accepted, what do I need to bring?

Need: Laptop and charger, cellphone and charger, sleeping bag, pillow, toiletries, a couple changes of clothes (nerdy shirts and onesies are particularly appreciated).
Don't need: a pre-determined team, an idea for an app, food or drink.
Please don't bring: Firearms or other weapons, alcohol, illegal drugs, animals (no matter how adorable).


Hacking


When/Where can I hack?

WaiHack will start in the evening of Friday, July 7th with the opening ceremony. A detailed schedule of events and locations will be posted later. All work eligible for prizes must be completed between Friday night after the opening ceremony and Sunday morning at the Ghallagher Academy of Performing Arts
.

What can I win?

It's not about winning—it's about learning and making friends that last for a lifetime (so everyone's a winner!). If you're thinking, "that's adorable...but really," then we've got you covered too. A panel of professional judges will select the best three hacks based on the following criteria: creativity, technical difficulty, polish, and usefulness. Top prizes are TBA, but usually involve tech and insane perks from company sponsors. Most sponsors offer their own prizes too for specific categories, which they judge and fund themselves.


Other


I'm not a student, so how can I get involved?

We will be allowing recent graduates or persons under the age of 25 to participate in the event. If you're looking to mentor (i.e. someone with technical skills who'd be willing to help out participants during the weekend), you should reach out to us. Otherwise, you can see the finished products at the demos on Sunday.

How is WaiHack run?

WaiHack is planned almost entirely by University of Waikato students. One hundred percent of the funding for WaiHack comes from corporate sponsor donations. You can find more information about sponsoring at our sponsor section.

What if this FAQ didn’t answer my questions?

Please email us at team@waihack.org if you’re in any way confused or concerned! We’d love to help you out :)

What should I do if I have additional questions?

If you have questions about anything, shoot us an email at team@waihack.org.

Where will I sleep?

Hacking all night is fun, but you can also get some rest when you need it! If you indicate in your confirmation form, we can match you with a Waikato University student who will provide places to sleep and shower.



Application


What's the application process like?

We're looking for creative and innovative people with a set of skills that can contribute to a project - mobile, web, hardware, design, or anything else really cool. We generally prefer people that have proven their abilities in previous projects, evident through links, project descriptions, Github profiles, video demos or awards.

When will applications close?

The application will close June 16th at 11:59pm.

Who can apply?

Any tech, university, recent grad, or high school student 15 years old or older on July 7th, 2017 is eligible to apply.

Should Waikato students fill out the application too?

Yes! Every Waikato student who wants to participate is free to, If you apply by June 16th and confirm by June 23rd, you will be guaranteed a spot at WaiHack automatically. Otherwise, you can try for walk-in admission the day of the event.
